ABOUT RUBBER ARMSTRONG

About Rubber Armstrong

Established 2015. Long-running camp. Experienced crew. Selective membership.

Background

Rubber Armstrong has operated at Burning Man since 2015. Solar-powered infrastructure. Self-sufficient systems. Participatory model.

The camp runs on contribution, not consumption. People who join understand this.

How the Camp Operates

Responsibility

People take care of each other. People take care of the camp. No one carries dead weight.

Consent

Ask first. Respect the answer. A "maybe" is a "no". Silence is not consent. This applies to everything.

Leave No Trace

Treat people, art, and space with care. Leave nothing behind. Honour the effort others put in.

Collective Operation

The camp cannot be run by a few people. Everyone plays their part. The group is measured as a whole.

Infrastructure

Systems designed for reliability in extreme conditions.

Solar Power

Off-grid solar powers the entire camp. Tent AC, art car, all infrastructure.

Water

5,000L filtered drinking water. Daily showers with eco-friendly soap.

Recycling

Everything consumed is taken out and recycled. Constant MOOP sweeps. The camp cleans up after itself and the area around it.

People are reminded when they miss something. This is normal.

Infrastructure That Works

Spacious, insulated tents with triple-layered thermal material and 120V power. Aluminium shade cloth that blocks UV while allowing hot air to escape. Playa-proven designs refined over years of iteration.

How We Operate

Participation, Not Passivity

We, as burners, are not individually critiqued but rather as a whole. Together we can improve our participation in Black Rock City, justify our placement, and honour those who have tirelessly worked before us to make our nine days of madness an achievable and desirable goal, year after year.

Everyone's small but meaningful contributions achieve our combined success.

No Passengers

Clean the solar panels. Work your bar shift. Cook dinner. Everyone contributes. This is not plug-and-play. This is not a hotel. This is a working camp.

Participation

People contribute. The camp operates because people take responsibility.

This Is Not for Everyone

If this approach makes sense, submit a Statement of Intent.
