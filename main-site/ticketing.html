Ticketing

Making the ticket situation crystal clear: the relationship between Burning Man tickets and Rubber Armstrong membership.

RA Does Not Sell Burning Man Tickets

This is critical to understand: Rubber Armstrong does not sell, distribute, or guarantee Burning Man tickets.

Burning Man tickets are purchased directly from Burning Man through their official ticket sales. Camp membership with RA is a separate matter entirely.

Two Independent Systems

  • Burning Man Ticketing: Managed by Burning Man organization, various sale categories (Main Sale, FOMO, etc.)
  • RA Camp Membership: Managed by Rubber Armstrong, based on values alignment and participation

Having a Burning Man ticket does not automatically guarantee you a spot in RA. Being accepted to RA does not guarantee you a Burning Man ticket.

Burning Man Ticket Overview

Burning Man tickets are sold through several channels throughout the year.

Main Sale

The primary general admission sale, typically held in the spring. Registration opens weeks in advance, and successful registrants receive a unique link to purchase tickets. This is a lottery-style system.

FOMO Sale (OMG Sale)

A secondary sale later in the year for those who didn't secure tickets in the main sale. Also lottery-based, typically with fewer tickets available.

Directed Group Sale (DGS)

Burning Man allocates a limited number of tickets to registered theme camps, art installations, and mutant vehicles that have been pre-approved. These tickets help ensure that placed camps and projects can fulfill their commitments to the city.

Other Categories

Vehicle passes, low-income tickets, and other specialized categories exist. Check the official Burning Man website for complete and current information.

Official source: All ticketing information, dates, and processes are published on burningman.org. This page provides a summary only.

How RA Handles Tickets

Directed Group Sale (DGS) Participation

Rubber Armstrong applies for Directed Group Sale tickets as a registered theme camp. If allocated tickets, these are distributed internally based on:

  • Returning core members and camp leadership
  • Build crew and key operational roles
  • Individuals with critical camp responsibilities
  • Length of involvement and contribution to camp

DGS allocation is limited and never covers the entire camp. Most members secure their own tickets through Main Sale or FOMO.

No Markup, No Unofficial Sales

RA does not mark up tickets. Any DGS tickets distributed are sold at face value. We do not run unofficial ticket sales or resales outside of Burning Man's official channels.

Ticket Requirements for Membership

To camp with Rubber Armstrong at Burning Man 2026, you will ultimately need a valid Burning Man ticket. However, you do not need a ticket to submit a Statement of Intent or begin the conversation with us.

What To Do

I Already Have a Ticket

Great! You're one step ahead. Here's what to do:

  1. Submit a Statement of Intent on our SOI form to express interest in RA
  2. Wait for review - we'll assess your background and fit
  3. If approved, you'll receive the "I Want to Join" form after ticket sales close
  4. Complete the operational form with ticket details, dates, and commitments

Submit Statement of Intent

I Don't Have a Ticket Yet

No problem. Most people are in this boat. Here's what to do:

  1. Submit a Statement of Intent anyway - get on our radar early
  2. Register for Main Sale through Burning Man's official channels
  3. Stay in touch with RA through the process
  4. If you secure a ticket and are approved by RA, you'll get the "I Want to Join" form

Note: RA cannot guarantee ticket access, but we can keep you informed of our DGS allocations if applicable.

Submit Statement of Intent

I Have a Spare Ticket

If you have an extra ticket and want it to go to an RA member:

  1. Do NOT advertise it publicly or sell above face value
  2. Contact RA leadership through official channels once our community is assembled
  3. We may be able to connect you with approved members who are still seeking tickets
  4. All transfers should follow Burning Man's official ticket transfer process

Ticket scalping violates Burning Man's terms and conditions. All transfers should be at face value only.

I'm Confused or Have Questions

Ticketing can be complex. Here's how to get clarity:

  1. Read the official Burning Man ticketing FAQ on burningman.org
  2. Submit your Statement of Intent and include questions in the form
  3. Reach out to RA with specific questions about our camp's ticket policies

Typical Timeline

Here's how ticketing and RA membership typically unfolds (dates vary by year):

  • January-March: Main Sale registration opens, RA accepts Statements of Intent
  • March-April: Main Sale occurs, RA reviews SOI submissions
  • May-June: FOMO sale, RA continues accepting SOI
  • June-July: Ticket sales close, RA releases "I Want to Join" form to approved members
  • August: Burning Man! Final logistics, camp setup, playa magic

These dates are approximate and change year to year. Check Burning Man's official site for current year information.
