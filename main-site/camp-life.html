DAY-TO-DAY AT RA

Camp Life

How the camp operates day-to-day. Shifts, systems, expectations.

Radiance Hour

Daily informal knowledge share. Skills, stories, practical hacks.

Yoga, sound work, bike maintenance, playa tricks, whatever people know. Runs during bar shift unless it needs the Bedouin tent. Bring your own props.

Format

No sign-ups. No microphones. Free-flow audience. Casual setting.

Share what you know. People appreciate it.

Shifts

The camp cannot be run by a few people. Everyone signs up for 1-2 shifts.

Roster managed by the Hen House. Sign up at Monday morning camp meeting.

The group is measured as a whole.

Shift Roles

Sign up at Monday meeting.

⌂ The Hen House

Camp logistics

  • • Allocation of tents and bikes
  • • Rostering changes
  • • General point of call for camp questions
  • • Facilitating Radiance Hour
  • • Good knowledge of nearby services and camps

◈ Cook Crews

Kitchen operations

  • • Put daily allocation out to defrost
  • • Warm cooking water totes
  • • Set up family dinner tables
  • • Prepare serving stations
  • • Boil water and cook freezer bags
  • • Clean kitchen - wash and dry

◉ Planeteers

Recycling and MOOP

  • • Sweep camping area, trailers, bedouin tent, art car
  • • Drain and crush all cans
  • • Empty bins and refresh bin bags
  • • MOOP sweep all areas
  • • Take cardboard to burn barrel camp

◇ Barbies

Lubricating Dusty Throats

  • • Get ice for the camp
  • • Take recycling to Recycling Camp
  • • Clean bar completely
  • • Prepare margie mix and coolers
  • • Bar shift from 3pm to sunset
  • • Clean bar equipment
  • • BYO camp DJ or share the time

◆ Art Car Drivers

Art car operation

  • • Must be sober and hold a driving license
  • • Shifts from 2-6 hours (day or night)
  • • 5 MPH speed limit, always
  • • Drive direct route to playa
  • • Responsible for state of charge
  • • Responsible for spotters

◈ Radiance Hour Experts

Knowledge sharing

Not a formal shift. Share what you know. Your call.

SS Mezcal: Art Car

Solar-powered mobile platform. Playa exploration and transport.

Respect the art, respect the dust, respect the driver.

Driver Requirements

Drivers must be sober. Spotters required in dust storms, busy areas, and streets. In whiteouts, double the spotters or stop.

Return It Clean

Wipe it down. Recycle cans. Refresh the cooler. Take your belongings.

Return the art car better than you found it.

Mezcal Bar

Communal bar. Main gift to the playa.

Camp placement is in a busy area. Most alcohol budget goes to the bar.

Everyone works bar shifts. People throw in communal beer every few days. Share what you have.

Meals

All dinners are vegetarian. Meals are pre-cooked and frozen to maintain hygiene and reduce waste.

Kitchen trailer has a double burner and cooking essentials. Free to use outside dinner hours. Clean up after yourself.

Working Camp

The group is measured as a whole. Placement depends on collective performance.

Clean the solar panels. Work your bar shift. Cook dinner. Everyone contributes.
