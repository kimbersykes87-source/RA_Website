Joining Rubber Armstrong

A deliberate, three-step process that separates alignment from commitment, intent from logistics, values from operations.

Why This Process?

Rubber Armstrong is not a turn-key camp. We're not a hotel. We're a working community built on shared values, mutual respect, and participatory effort.

Our joining process reflects this. We want to know who you are before we talk about logistics. We want to understand your values before we discuss your ticket status. We want to establish alignment before we make commitments.

"The separation of intent vs commitment is deliberate and non-negotiable."

Common Questions

Do I need a Burning Man ticket to submit a Statement of Intent?

No. You do not need a ticket to submit SOI. In fact, we encourage you to submit SOI before securing a ticket. This gets you on our radar early and helps us plan capacity.

If I'm approved for SOI, am I guaranteed a spot in RA?

Not yet. SOI approval means we think you're a good values fit. But you still need to complete the "I Want to Join" form after ticket sales, and you need a Burning Man ticket. Final confirmation happens after all operational details are settled.

Can I submit SOI if I'm not sure I'll get a ticket?

Yes, absolutely. Most people submit SOI before knowing their ticket status. If you're approved and later can't secure a ticket, we understand. If you secure a ticket but aren't approved for RA, you can camp elsewhere.

What happens if I submit SOI and am not approved?

We'll let you know. Approval is based on values alignment, camp capacity, and fit with our community. Not being approved doesn't mean you're "rejected" as a person ‚Äî it just means RA might not be the right camp match for you this year.

How long does the review take?

Typically 2-4 weeks, but this varies based on submission volume and time of year. We review all SOIs manually. You'll hear from us either way.

When does the "I Want to Join" form open?

After Burning Man ticket sales close (typically June-July). At that point, approved SOI applicants receive a direct link. This timing ensures we're talking about real commitments, not hypotheticals.

What We're Looking For

Rubber Armstrong is built on specific values. When we review Statements of Intent, we're looking for alignment with:

  • Participation: You understand this is a working camp, not a hotel
  • Respect: For people, art, space, and effort
  • Consent: Asking first, respecting answers, every time
  • Community: All for one and one for all
  • Sustainability: Leave no trace, minimal environmental impact
  • Contribution: Everyone brings something, everyone does something

We're not looking for perfection. We're looking for people who get it, who want to be part of something collaborative, who understand that camps like RA only work because everyone gives a damn.
